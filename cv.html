Biography

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction, known for his work on user-centered design, usability, and cognitive engineering. His book The Design of Everyday Things (1988) introduced the Action Cycle and the gulfs of execution and evaluation – foundational concepts for understanding interaction.

Norman's work bridges cognitive psychology and engineering, emphasizing that design should accommodate human capabilities and limitations. His concepts are central to Dix et al. Chapter 9 (Cognitive Frameworks) and inform the evaluation methods in Chapter 12.

Key Contributions to HCI

Table 1: Don Norman's major intellectual contributions and their relevance to HCI
Year Contribution Description Relevance to Dix Ch.9 & 12
1986 User-Centered System Design (with Draper) Advocated for designing systems around user needs, goals, and tasks rather than technical constraints. Foundations of task analysis and user-centered design (Ch.9)
1988 The Design of Everyday Things
Action Cycle & Gulfs		 Introduced the seven-stage action cycle: goal, plan, specify, execute, perceive, interpret, evaluate. Defined gulfs of execution and evaluation. Direct source for Norman's model in Ch.9 (pp. 161-165)
1990s Information Appliances Conceptualized appliances designed for single, focused purposes rather than general-purpose computers. GOMS application domains and task-specific interfaces (Ch.12)
1998 The Invisible Computer Argued that technology should become "invisible" – focused on tasks, not computers themselves. Relates to evaluation gulf and transparency in interaction

Academic & Professional Positions

University of California, San Diego

Professor Emeritus, Cognitive Science & Psychology (1966–1993)

Established one of the first HCI programs. Founded the Department of Cognitive Science.

Apple Computer, Inc.

Fellow & Vice President of Advanced Technology (1993–1997)

Served as "User Experience Architect" – first executive-level UX role in major technology company. Advocated for user-centered design in product development.

Nielsen Norman Group

Co-founder & Principal Consultant (1998–present)

Co-founded with Jakob Nielsen. Consulting and advocacy for usability and evidence-based design.

Northwestern University

Professor, Computer Science (2001–2010)

Directed the Segal Design Institute, advancing design education in engineering.
