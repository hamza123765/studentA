Norman vs. GOMS: Comparative Framework

This analysis applies both models to the task of enabling Wi-Fi on a smartphone. The table below identifies conceptual alignments and fundamental divergences between Norman’s cognitive framework and the GOMS engineering model.

Analytical Summary

Norman’s model provides a complete cognitive cycle accounting for perception, interpretation, and evaluation – essential for identifying usability gulfs.

GOMS is an engineering model that predicts expert, error-free execution time by focusing on goals, methods, and operators, deliberately abstracting away perceptual and evaluative stages.

KLM further simplifies GOMS to six operators for rapid execution-time prediction, adding mental operators [M] but still omitting interpretation and evaluation.
